Tech IMPACT is LaGuardia Community College’s Division of Adult and Continuing Education’s no-cost web development training program for eligible young adults, aged 17-29. Tech IMPACT students learn programming fundamentals, product development, and web development to prepare for jobs as front-end web developers. Classes begin in February, 2017. Training will take place at LaGuardia and in General Assembly’s Web Development Immersive program. To see if you are eligible, and to begin the application process, please go to our online application.

Open Code is a five course web development training program that will teach you how to create web sites using programming languages, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript and PHP.

Instructors

Johan Martin

Johan Martin has worked on websites for companies such as Rent The Runway, CNBC and Experian working on both the front-end and back-end of their web projects. He spends most of his time in New York City but has been known to spend time in locations as far-flung as Bali to South Africa. He just completed a project as the photographer for a book on the Wineries of the Sierra Foot Hills

Caridad Munoz

Caridad Munoz is the Manager for Creative and Web Services in the Marketing and Communications Department at LaGuardia Community College. Caridad’s passion and focus is in bringing humanity to digital experiences through solutions that serve a real, tangible purpose to people and businesses. Her expertise is derived from over 10 years of creating compelling designs, developing and maintaining websites, and a broad range of online initiatives.

Caridad is also an Adjunct Instructor in LaGuardia’s Humanities Department where she teaches Internet and Mobile Game Design with ActionScript, Web Development & Design Foundation with HTML 5, CSS3, Framework, Internet Video and DVD Development using Adobe Creative Suite.
